About In-Body Targets

Below are descriptions of the in-body targets that you can target with your synthetic nucleic acid! Click "Learn more" for more information on each target.

Ochratoxin A

Ochratoxin A is a foodborne mycotoxin that can contaminate improperly stored foodstuffs, most commonly various grains, coffee, or wine. When ingested, Ochratoxin A targets the kidneys and can lead to a variety of kidney diseases. Ochratoxin A is a proven carcinogen in animals and a potential human carcinogen.

Learn more »

Vomitoxin

Vomitoxin is a mycotoxin that contaminates wheat, corn, and other grains. Upon ingestion, vomitoxin can cause nausea, vomiting, headache, and fever, due to interactions with dopamine receptors in the brain.

Learn more »

Tumor Necrosis Factor

Tumor necrosis factor (TNF) is a signal protein used by the immune system to induce inflammation. Excess TNF has been linked to diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis and Crohn's Disease as well as certain types of cancers.

Learn more »

Interferon-α

Interferon-α is a signaling protein involved in fighting off viral infections. High levels of the protein have been found in many patients with type-1 diabetes, and it is believed that Interferon-α plays a role in the onset of diabetes.

Learn more »

Interleukin-1β

Interleukin-1β is a cytokine that regulates inflammation. An excess of Interleukin-1β can lead to cryopyrin-associated periodic syndrome (CAPS), an autoinflammatory disease affecting the skin, joints, and nervous system. High levels of Interleukin-1β in cancer patients has been linked to tumor aggression and invasiveness.

Learn more »

Protein Tyrosine Kinase-7

Protein Tyrosine Kinase-7 is a receptor protein that is commonly overexpressed in many cancer patients. PTK7 has been linked to colorectal, gastric, cervical, and skin cancers.

Learn more »

Cell Surface Nucleolin

Cell surface nucleolin is a protein found on the extracellular side of certain lymphocytes and cancer cells. It is essential to cell growth and differentiation, but also acts as a receptor for many molecules linked to tumor formation and the spread of cancer.

Learn more »

Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor

Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor (VEGF) is a protein responsible for the production of new blood vessels. The production of too many blood vessels can lead to a wide variety of complications in different parts of the body. For example, excess VEGF in the heart can lead to defects during development and excess VEGF in the eyes can lead to blindness.

Learn more »

HIV-1 Integrase

HIV-1 integrase is a protein used by the HIV virus to incorporate viral DNA into its target’s genome. It is crucial to the infection and spread of HIV. Inhibiting the action of HIV-1 integrase has seen some success as an HIV therapy.

Learn more »

Janus Kinase-1

Janus Kinase-1 (JAK1) is a signaling protein critical for many immune response pathways. An excess of JAK1 can lead to rheumatoid arthritis and psoriasis. JAK inhibitors have also been used to regrow hair in patients with alopecia.

Learn more »
