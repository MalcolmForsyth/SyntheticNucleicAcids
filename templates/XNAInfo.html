About XNAs

Also known as xeno nucleic acids (XNAs), synthetic nucleic acids are nucleic acids that have been modified to have a different sugar backbone. These modifications allow the XNAs to bind better to certain in-body targets, allowing for better targeting for toxins or signaling proteins. Below are each of the modified synthetic acids and their structures.

Cyclohexene Nucleic Acid (CeNA)

Glycol Nucleic Acid (GNA)

Threose Nucleic Acid (TNA)

Arabino Nucleic Acid (ANA)

Fluoro Arabino Nucleic Acid (FANA)

Peptide Nucleic Acid (PNA)

Locked Nucleic Acid (LNA)
