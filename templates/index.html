Welcome to EOH!

Engineering Open House is an annual event where students demonstrate their creativity and technical skill through a variety of exhibits. This year marks the 100th anniversary of EOH, and though unprecedented events have caused it to be a little different this year, we still hope to create a memorable and impactful experience!

Our Exhibit

In recent years, scientists have been developing synthetic nucleic acids, called Xeno Nucleic Acids (XNA), which function like DNA and RNA, but are spliced with a completely different structural backbone to serve human purposes. Utilizing the unique structure of XNA, researchers are looking to solve biomolecular problems, protect biodiversity, and cure degenerative diseases. In this exhibit, we’ll explore the development of XNA, and you’ll get to build your own nucleic acid and examine its properties.
